# The Severance Maximizer Script Kit

HR's primary goal is to close your file quickly and cheaply. Your goal is to maximize your financial runway while protecting your professional reputation.

This guide provides the framework and exact scripts to securely negotiate your severance.

## 1. The "Big Negotiables" (What You Can Actually Ask For)

Most professionals think they can only negotiate weeks of pay. In reality, there are 11 common leverage points. When evaluating your offer, consider asking for:

1. **Additional Weeks of Base Pay:** (Standard is 1-2 weeks per year of service. Asking for an extra 4 weeks is common.)
2. **COBRA Reimbursement:** (Ask the company to pay your health insurance premiums for 1-3 months.)
3. **Pro-rated Bonuses / Commission:** (If you were on track for a bonus, ask for the pro-rated amount up to your termination date.)
4. **Vesting Acceleration:** (If your equity vests in 30 days, ask them to accelerate it or extend your termination date.)
5. **Unused PTO Payout:** (If not mandated by your state, ask for it explicitly.)
6. **Outplacement Services:** (Company pays for career coaching / resume writing.)
7. **Retaining Company Property:** (Ask to keep your laptop or phone after it is wiped.)
8. **Mutual Non-Disparagement:** (Ensure the company is legally barred from badmouthing you, not just the other way around.)
9. **Neutral Reference Guarantee:** (Written confirmation that they will only verify titles/dates.)
10. **Removal of Non-Compete Clauses:** (Particularly if you need to find work in your industry immediately to survive.)
11. **Extension of Exercise Window:** (For stock options, extending the standard 90-day window to 1-2 years.)

## 2. The Negotiation Scripts

### Script A: The "Soft Stall"
*Use this when HR hands you the agreement and asks you to sign it.*

**Spoken / Email:**
> "Thank you for providing the documentation. This is a lot to process. I am going to take a few days to review the agreement and consult with my family [and/or advisor] before returning it. I appreciate your patience."

### Script B: The Rebuttal Email
*Use this to formally ask for a better package. Keep it professional, objective, and unemotional.*

**Subject:** Severance Agreement Review - [Your Name]

**Body:**
> Hi [HR Rep Name],
> 
> Thank you for sharing the separation agreement. I have reviewed the terms.
> 
> Given my [Number]-year tenure at the company, my recent "Exceeds Expectations" performance review, and the successful delivery of [Specific Project Name], I am hoping we can find a slightly more equitable arrangement that reflects my contributions.
> 
> I would like to request the following adjustments before signing the release:
> 
> 1. An increase in severance pay from [X] weeks to [Y] weeks.
> 2. The company covering my COBRA premiums for [Z] months.
> 3. Confirmation of a mutual non-disparagement clause. 
> 
> If we can update the agreement to reflect these three adjustments, I am prepared to sign immediately to ensure a smooth and amicable transition. 
> 
> Thank you for considering this request.
> 
> Best regards,
> [Your Name]

### Script C: Handling the "Non-Negotiable" Pushback
*If HR replies that the package is "standard" and "non-negotiable."*

**Spoken / Email:**
> "I understand the company has a standard baseline. However, individual contributions vary, and I believe my specific track record—including [insert 1 high value metric]—warrants an exception. Even if base pay cannot be adjusted, can we find flexibility on the COBRA reimbursement or accelerating my vesting date? I want to get this signed, but I need us to find a middle ground."

## 3. The Golden Rules of Negotiation

1. **Be Reasonable:** Asking for 12 months of severance after 2 years of work will end the conversation. Asking for 2 more weeks and 1 month of COBRA is highly defensible.
2. **Never threaten legal action:** Unless you have an actual employment attorney retained and a valid discrimination/wrongful termination claim, throwing out legal threats shuts down negotiations and sends your file directly to corporate counsel.
3. **Offer finality:** Employers negotiate to eliminate risk. Always end your ask with: *"If we can agree to this, I will sign immediately."*
