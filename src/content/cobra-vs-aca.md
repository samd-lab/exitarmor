# Health Insurance Decision Matrix: COBRA vs. ACA

Losing employer-sponsored health insurance is one of the most frightening aspects of a layoff. Fortunately, you have two primary safety nets. You must decide within **60 days**.

## Option A: COBRA (The Extender)
COBRA allows you to keep your exact same employer-provided health insurance plan for up to 18-36 months. 
* **The Catch:** You now pay 100% of the premium (plus a 2% administrative fee). If your employer subsidized heavily, your premium will skyrocket (often $400-$800/mo for individuals, $1,500+/mo for families).
* **The Rule:** You have exactly **60 days** from the date you lose coverage (or receive the COBRA notice, whichever is later) to elect it. 
* **The Payment Trap:** Once elected, you only have **45 days** to make your first premium payment. If you miss this, you permanently lose the right to the coverage.

## Option B: The ACA Marketplace (Obamacare)
Losing job-based coverage triggers a **Special Enrollment Period** allowing you to buy a private plan on HealthCare.gov.
* **The Catch:** You will be changing plans, meaning your deductible resets and you must check if your preferred doctors are in-network.
* **The Benefit:** Based on your new, reduced income (unemployment), you will likely qualify for **Premium Tax Credits**, which severely subsidize the monthly cost—sometimes dropping premiums to under $50/month.
* **The Rule:** You have **60 days** from the date your coverage ends to elect an ACA plan.

## The Decision Matrix

| Your Situation | Best Choice | Why |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Mid-Medical Treatment / Pregnant** | **COBRA** | Keeps your current doctors, network, and out-of-pocket maximum intact. Continuity of care is critical. |
| **Tight Budget / Healthy** | **ACA Marketplace** | Premium tax credits based on your reduced income can make ACA substantially cheaper than COBRA. |
| **Spouse has Insurance** | **Spouse’s Plan** | Your layoff qualifies as a "Life Event," allowing you to immediately jump on your spouse or partner’s plan. Usually the cheapest option. |
| **Employer offered to pay premiums** | **COBRA** | If you negotiated 3 months of COBRA coverage in your severance, ride it out for free, then switch to ACA. |

## The Retroactive Hack (The 59-Day Rule)
COBRA coverage is retroactive to the day you lost your original coverage. 
If you are perfectly healthy, some individuals use the 60-day election window as a "float" period. If they get a new job with benefits by Day 50, they never elect or pay for COBRA, saving thousands. If they get into an accident on Day 45, they immediately elect COBRA, pay the premium, and the hospital bills are completely covered retroactively. 

*Warning: This requires having the cash reserve to pay the retroactive premiums instantly if an emergency happens.*
