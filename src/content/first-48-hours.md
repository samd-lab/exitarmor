# The First 48 Hours: Your Immediate Survival Checklist

Getting laid off is a shock to the system. Most people make their biggest financial mistakes within the first 48 hours because they are operating in "panic mode." 

Your goal right now is **stabilization**. Do not rush. Follow this checklist step-by-step.

## DAY 1: Defense & Securing Your Perimeter (The First 6 Hours)

- [ ] **Do NOT sign your severance agreement.**
  HR will likely ask you to sign your release immediately. **Do not do it.** Under federal law (ADEA), if you are over 40, you have 21 days to review the document. If you are under 40, there is no federal minimum, but you still have the right to ask for 3–5 days to review it. Tell them: *"I need a few days to review this with my family/advisor."*
- [ ] **Confirm your exact "Termination Date."**
  Ask HR immediately: *"What is my official last day of employment on paper?"* This date dictates when your benefits end and when your COBRA window begins.
- [ ] **Confirm your Health Insurance End Date.**
  Ask HR: *"Does my health insurance end at midnight tonight, or at the end of this calendar month?"* Most companies carry coverage through the end of the month, but you must verify.
- [ ] **Export your personal data (If allowed).**
  If you still have access to your machine, forward any non-proprietary performance reviews, pay stubs, and personal contacts to your private email. **Do not forward company code, client lists, or intellectual property—this violates your NDA and jeopardizes your severance.**
- [ ] **Change your 401(k) / Fidelity / Vanguard email.**
  If your retirement accounts are linked to your work email, log in from a personal device and change the contact email immediately.

## DAY 2: Locking in Your Money (The Next 24 Hours)

- [ ] **File for Unemployment IMMEDIATELY.**
  Do not wait until you have "figured things out." There is no federal deadline for starting an unemployment claim, but state benefits **do not backpay to the day you were laid off** if you delay filing. File today to lock in your effective claim date. 
  *Note:* When asked for the reason for separation, use the exact phrase **"Laid off / Position Eliminated."**
- [ ] **Calculate your "Bleed Rate."**
  Look at your last 3 months of bank statements. Calculate your absolute minimum monthly survival number (Rent + Groceries + Utilities + Minimum Debt Payments). Cut all non-essential subscriptions today.
- [ ] **Check your state's PTO Payout Law.**
  If you live in CA, CO, IL, MA, MT, NE, or ND, your employer is legally required to pay you for unused vacation time. Verify that this amount is itemized in your final paycheck or severance offer.
- [ ] **Request a Neutral Reference.**
  Email HR and ask them to confirm in writing that the company's official policy for future background checks is a "neutral reference" (verifying only dates of employment and job title). This protects you from a former manager giving a bad reference.
- [ ] **Breathe.**
  You have legally secured your position. Your unemployment clock has started. Your benefits are documented. Now you are ready to review your severance offer.
