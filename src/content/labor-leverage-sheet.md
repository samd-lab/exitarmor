# State-by-State Labor Leverage Sheet

Employment laws vary wildly depending on what state you reside in. What is illegal in California might be standard practice in Texas. Understanding your specific state laws gives you significant leverage in severance negotiations.

## The Big Three (Largest Labor Markets)

### CALIFORNIA ☀️
*The most employee-friendly state in the US.*
* **Unused PTO Payout:** **Required by Law.** Vacation time is considered earned wages. "Use-it-or-lose-it" policies are strictly illegal. Your employer must pay out all accrued, unused time in your final paycheck.
* **WARN Act (Mini-WARN):** Covers mass layoffs at companies with **75 or more employees** (stricter than the federal 100-employee limit), requiring 60 days' notice.
* **Non-Competes:** Completely unenforceable and illegal. If HR tries to slip a non-compete into your severance, it is void.
* **Unemployment Filing:** [edd.ca.gov](https://edd.ca.gov)

### NEW YORK 🗽
*Highly protective, with strict notification rules.*
* **Unused PTO Payout:** **Required (with a catch).** Must be paid out *unless* the employer has a very clear, explicitly written policy stating that it is forfeited upon termination. If the handbook is silent, you get paid.
* **WARN Act:** New York requires **90 days' notice** for mass layoffs (stricter than the federal 60 days). If they violate this, you are owed hefty backpay.
* **Unemployment Filing:** [labor.ny.gov](https://labor.ny.gov)

### TEXAS 🤠
*Employer-friendly, heavy reliance on company policy.*
* **Unused PTO Payout:** **Not Required by Law.** Texas defers entirely to the company's written handbook. If the policy says you forfeit PTO, you lose it. *Leverage Strategy: Ask for it as part of your severance ask if policy denies it.*
* **WARN Act:** Defers to the federal baseline (100+ employees, 60 days' notice).
* **Unemployment Filing:** [twc.texas.gov](https://twc.texas.gov)

## Other Major Markets at a Glance

* **Florida:** No mandated PTO payout (depends on company policy). Unemployment maxes out at a very low ~$275/week. File at [floridajobs.org](https://floridajobs.org).
* **Illinois:** PTO payout is **required** by law. File at [ides.illinois.gov](https://ides.illinois.gov).
* **Massachusetts:** PTO payout is **required** by law. Non-competes are heavily restricted.
* **New Jersey:** The only state that currently **mandates** severance pay (1 week per year of service) for qualified mass layoffs.

## The Federal Baseline (Applies Everywhere)
* **WARN Act:** Companies with 100+ full-time employees must give 60 days' notice of a mass layoff. If they fail to do so, they owe you 60 days of backpay and benefits.
* **ADEA (Workers over 40):** You legally have 21 days to consider a severance offer and 7 days to revoke your signature federally. Do not let HR pressure you into same-day signing.

*Disclaimer: Employment law is inherently complex and subject to change. Always consult your state's Department of Labor website or a licensed employment attorney to verify current regulations.*
