# The 90-Day Defense Budget Guide

When your income stops, your financial strategy shifts from "Growth" to "Defense." You need to calculate your **Runway** (how many months you can survive without income) and your **Bleed Rate** (the absolute minimum amount of cash you need to burn each month).

## Step 1: Calculate Your "Bleed Rate"
Forget your current budget. A Defense Budget strips away everything except the 4 Walls:
1. **Housing:** Rent or Mortgage.
2. **Food:** Groceries (No takeout, no delivery).
3. **Utilities:** Electricity, Water, Internet, Phone.
4. **Transportation:** Car payment, insurance, gas (only for essentials).
*Plus: Minimum Debt Payments and Health Insurance.*

Everything else—Netflix, gym memberships, dining out—must be paused immediately. You can always turn them back on in a month if you feel secure, but cut them today.

## Step 2: Negotiate Your Fixed Bills
Creditors want to get paid. If you let them know you've been laid off *before* you miss a payment, many have "hardship forbearance" programs.

### Script for Credit Cards & Auto Loans
> "Hi, I've recently been impacted by a company layoff. I have an excellent payment history with you, but I need to protect my cash reserves for the next 60 days while I secure my next role. Do you offer a hardship forbearance or the ability to defer my payment to the end of my loan term without penalty?"

*If you have federal student loans, apply for Income-Driven Repayment (IDR) or an Unemployment Deferment immediately at studentaid.gov.*

## Step 3: Map Your Runway
Add up your total liquid cash (Checking + Savings + Severance + Estimated Unemployment Pay). Divide that total by your Monthly Bleed Rate. 
*Example: You have $15,000 liquid. Your Bleed Rate is $3,000/mo. Your Runway is 5 Months.*

Knowing this number reduces panic. It tells you exactly how much time you have to find the *right* job, rather than taking the *first* job out of desperation. 

## Step 4: The 401(k) Warning
**Do NOT cash out your 401(k) or IRA.** 
Unless you are facing imminent eviction or bankruptcy, cashing out your retirement is the most expensive money you can access. Between the 10% early withdrawal penalty and standard income taxes, you will lose 30%–40% of the money the instant you touch it. Leave it alone. Consider a personal loan or 0% APR credit card before raiding your 401(k).
